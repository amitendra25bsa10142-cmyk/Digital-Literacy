A situation where poor digital communication caused a real problem
can be seen in group projects. For example, a team of students was
working on an assignment but relied only on short text messages in a
chat group. One member misunderstood the deadline and submitted
their part late, which affected the entire group’s grade. The lack of
clear instructions and confirmation led to confusion and
miscommunication.
This problem could have been avoided by using clearer
communication methods. The group could have shared detailed
instructions, confirmed deadlines with all members, and used tools like
shared documents or scheduled online meetings. Regular updates
and proper coordination would have ensured that everyone was on
the same page. This shows the importance of clear, timely, and
effective digital communication in avoiding misunderstandings.
